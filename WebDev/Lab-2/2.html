Chandrayaan-3, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in July 2023, is India's third mission to the Moon. Its primary goal is to achieve a successful soft landing on the lunar surface, particularly near the south pole, an area of significant scientific interest due to potential water ice deposits. The mission includes a lander, Vikram, and a rover, Pragyan, designed to explore and send back data about the Moon's geology, mineral composition, and atmosphere. Chandrayaan-3 builds on the learnings from Chandrayaan-1 and -2, positioning India as a key player in lunar exploration.

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, is a historic Mughal-era fortification and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built between 1638 and 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan, it served as the Mughal emperor's main residence for around 200 years. The fort's name comes from its massive red sandstone walls, which stand at about 33 meters tall. Inside, visitors can explore stunning structures like the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the beautiful Moti Masjid. The fort also houses several museums. A symbol of India's rich cultural heritage, the Red Fort holds national significance, with Independence Day celebrations held here annually.

India won the Cricket World Cup for the first time in 1983. The historic victory took place on June 25, 1983, when India, led by captain Kapil Dev, defeated the West Indies at Lord's Cricket Ground in London. India won by 43 runs, scoring 183 runs, and bowling out the West Indies for 140. This victory marked a turning point for Indian cricket, inspiring a generation of cricketers and fans. It remains one of the most iconic moments in Indian sports history.
